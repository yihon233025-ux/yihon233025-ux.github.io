Shan Ze

Shan Ze (born 1991), whose real name is Li Yifan, is a Chinese artist known for his traditional-style aesthetic paintings. He was born in Luzhou, Sichuan, and graduated in 2013 from the College of Arts, Sichuan University. His representative works include "Guan Shan Hai" (Observing Mountains and Seas), "Luo Huang Ji", and "Black and White Painting Aesthetics". He is currently working on the series “Hundred Ghosts of China” and “Divine Spirits and Myths.” Shan Ze admires Tang dynasty painter Wu Daozi, as well as Dong Yuan and Li Cheng. His works are imbued with Eastern aesthetics, blending traditional themes with modern interpretations and ideas, offering new perspectives on mythical figures found in classical literature. The mythical creatures in his paintings are grand, romantic, elegant, and filled with boundless imagination. Shan Ze employs traditional and rustic artistic techniques, which can make his art somewhat challenging for the general public to appreciate. In contrast, most familiar depictions of monsters and spirits today are shaped by modern visual culture, influenced heavily by Western art and Japanese media. Audiences often prefer realistic, three-dimensional effects and strong visual impact, or gravitate toward the anime-style (two-dimensional) representations of such creatures.
